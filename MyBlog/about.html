01 : About me

Ciara Norton

Growing up on Vancouver Island I developped a passion for non-human nature, exploring the island's beautiful temperate raindforests. I am currently completing a geography degree in the Environment and Sustainability program at UBC. Throughout my degree and evolution as a geographer, I developped an aptitude for spatial thinking and further pursues a minor in GIS and Geographical computation. This passion gree into the UBC Hotmapper club and later evolved to me founding UBC's first GIS club as president, where myself and other members of the club shared our passion forr GIS through various workshops and events.

Research interests

As a geographer fascinated by the way environmental phenomena spatially unfold over time on Earth's dynamic landscape, I seek to understand how the non-human members of our ecosystem respond and adapt to climate change in a spatial context.
